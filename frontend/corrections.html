Corrections Policy

ANN Verify is committed to correcting errors promptly and transparently. We do not delete fact-checks — we correct them, label them, and preserve the public record for accountability.

// 01

Our Commitment to Accuracy

No fact-checking system is infallible. ANN Verify uses a 7-layer AI analysis pipeline and real-time evidence retrieval — but new information emerges, sources update, and human review can occasionally produce errors.

When mistakes occur, we correct them openly and without delay. Every correction is logged publicly. No fact-check is silently deleted or altered without disclosure.

"The integrity of the correction process is more important than protecting the original report. Transparency in error — not the absence of error — is the measure of a trustworthy fact-checker."
// 02

What We Correct

Three types of corrections, each with a distinct process and labeling requirement.

🔴
MAJOR CORRECTION
The verdict itself changes — e.g., FALSE → MOSTLY FALSE. Requires full editorial re-review before publication.
Example: "Original verdict: FALSE → Updated: MOSTLY FALSE — New evidence changes the evidentiary balance."
🟡
MINOR CORRECTION
Factual detail errors — incorrect dates, figures, names, or source attributions — where the overall verdict remains unchanged.
Example: "The report was released in 2023 [Edited: 2024 — April 2, 2026]"
🟢
EDITOR'S NOTE
No factual error, but important context has emerged. Original text preserved; a note is appended without changing the verdict.
Example: "Editor's Note (May 10, 2026): A subsequent study provides additional context. Verdict remains unchanged."

Review Process

All correction requests follow a structured editorial review process.

1
Receipt Acknowledgment
Automated confirmation sent immediately. Personal follow-up if email provided.
⏱ Within 24 hours
2
Editorial Review
Team independently verifies the claimed error using primary sources. Major corrections require a second reviewer.
⏱ Within 5 business days
3
Decision & Notification
We notify the requester — accepted or rejected — with a clear explanation.
⏱ Same day as decision
4
Publication & Log Entry
If accepted, the correction is applied with the appropriate label and a public log entry is added with a timestamp.
⏱ Within 24 hours of decision
// 05

How Corrections Are Labeled

All corrections are applied to the original fact-check with visible, dated labels. We never silently alter text.

  EXAMPLE — Major Correction label (top of fact-check article)
🔴 CORRECTION — Updated April 10, 2026
This fact-check has been updated following new evidence.
Original verdict: FALSE  →  Updated verdict: MOSTLY FALSE
Reason: Congressional testimony published April 8, 2026 introduced context not available at the time of original analysis.
// Minor Correction (inline):
"The regulation took effect in January 2023 [Edited: February 2023 — April 2, 2026], applying to..."
