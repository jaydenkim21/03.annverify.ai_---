Why Blockchain for Fact-Checking?

Traditional verification can be edited, deleted, or disputed. Blockchain removes that vulnerability.

Immutable Records

Once a fact-check is written to the blockchain, it cannot be modified or deleted — not even by us.

Fully Transparent

Every record is publicly verifiable. Anyone can confirm a fact-check result without relying on a central authority.

Decentralized Trust

No single entity controls the record. Trust is distributed across the network, eliminating single points of failure.

How Verification is Recorded

1

Claim Verified

AI analysis completes and a trust score is assigned to the claim.

2

Hash Generated

A cryptographic hash of the result is created — a unique fingerprint of that verification.

3

Written to Chain

The hash is written to the blockchain, creating a permanent timestamped record.
